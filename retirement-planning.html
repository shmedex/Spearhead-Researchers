Retirement Milestones

Key stages in your retirement planning journey

1

Early Career (20s-30s)

  • Start contributing to 401(k)/IRA
  • Establish emergency fund
  • Pay down high-interest debt
  • Develop investment strategy
2

Mid-Career (40s-50s)

  • Maximize retirement contributions
  • Diversify investment portfolio
  • Consider catch-up contributions
  • Estimate retirement expenses
3

Pre-Retirement (50s-60s)

  • Fine-tune asset allocation
  • Understand Social Security options
  • Plan healthcare coverage
  • Create withdrawal strategy
4

Retirement (65+)

  • Implement withdrawal plan
  • Manage required distributions
  • Adjust for inflation
  • Consider legacy planning

Retirement Strategies

Approaches to help you achieve your retirement goals

The 4% Rule

Withdraw 4% of your retirement portfolio in the first year, then adjust for inflation each subsequent year.

  • Pros: Simple to implement, historically sustainable
  • Cons: Doesn't account for market volatility early in retirement
Learn More

Bucket Strategy

Divide your assets into three buckets based on when you'll need the money (short-term, medium-term, long-term).

  • Pros: Reduces sequence risk, provides liquidity
  • Cons: More complex to manage
Learn More

Dynamic Withdrawals

Adjust withdrawals based on portfolio performance and market conditions each year.

  • Pros: Flexible, responds to market changes
  • Cons: Requires active management, variable income
Learn More

Home Equity Utilization

Use home equity through downsizing, reverse mortgages, or HELOCs to fund retirement.

  • Pros: Accesses illiquid assets, preserves investments
  • Cons: Reduces legacy, may have high costs
Learn More

Annuity Ladder

Purchase multiple annuities over time to create guaranteed income streams at different ages.

  • Pros: Guaranteed income, longevity protection
  • Cons: Less liquidity, potentially lower returns
Learn More

Total Return Approach

Withdraw from a balanced portfolio based on total returns (dividends + capital gains).

  • Pros: Portfolio continues to grow, flexible
  • Cons: Requires discipline, market risk
Learn More
